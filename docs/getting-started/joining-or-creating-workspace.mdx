# Joining or Creating a Workspace

In frndOS, a **workspace** is the top-level container for everything your team does — brands, projects, assets, and people. Every account belongs to at least one workspace.

Understanding workspaces is the second key step after creating your account (the first is [signing up](./creating-account)).

## What is a Workspace?

A workspace is where your team's work lives. Each workspace has:

- Its own **brands**, **projects**, and **assets**
- Its own **team members** with assigned roles
- Its own **subscription plan** and **credit pool**
- Its own **settings** (including visibility and admin controls)

> **Important:** Each workspace requires **its own subscription**. If you create multiple workspaces, you'll need to subscribe to a plan for each one separately.

## Two Paths: Join or Create

When you log in for the first time, you'll see two options.

### Option 1 — Join an Existing Workspace

This is the right path if your agency or organization already has a frndOS workspace.

### Option 2 — Create Your Own Workspace

Choose this if you're starting fresh, or if you want a separate workspace from your organization's main one.

## Joining an Existing Workspace

### Step 1 — Search for the Workspace

After signing in, use the **workspace search** to find your team's workspace by name.

> **Heads-up:** Workspaces are **not discoverable by default**. If you don't see your workspace in the search results, it means the workspace admin hasn't enabled public visibility.
>
> In that case, ask your admin to either:
> - Send you a direct invitation (see [Inviting Team Members](../workspace/inviting-team-members))
> - Or enable workspace visibility (see [Setting Workspace Visibility](../workspace/workspace-visibility))

### Step 2 — Request to Join

When you find the workspace, click **Request to Join**. Your request is sent to the workspace admin for approval.

### Step 3 — Wait for Approval

The admin will see your request as a notification in frndOS. Once they approve, you're automatically added to the workspace and can start using it.

👉 See the admin perspective: [Approving Join Requests](../workspace/approving-join-requests)

## Creating a Workspace

### When to Create vs. Join

| Create a new workspace when… | Join existing when… |
|---|---|
| You're an independent agency or studio | Your organization already has one |
| You want a separate billing or team scope | You'll collaborate with existing team members |
| You're testing frndOS for your team | You were invited by an admin |

### Step 1 — Choose "Create Workspace"

From the sign-up flow (or from the workspace switcher later), select **Create Workspace**.

### Step 2 — Configure the Workspace

Give the workspace a name and any other required settings.

### Step 3 — You Become the Owner

When you create a workspace, you automatically become its **owner**. As the owner, you can:

- **Invite team members** to join (see [Inviting Team Members](../workspace/inviting-team-members))
- **Subscribe to a plan** to unlock features and credits
- **Manage workspace settings** like visibility, branding, and permissions

### Step 4 — Subscribe

A new workspace needs an active subscription before you can use the creative tools. Choose a plan that fits your team's needs.

## Switching Between Workspaces

If you're part of multiple workspaces (for example, your agency's workspace plus a personal one), you can switch between them at any time using the workspace switcher in the top-left of the dashboard.

Each workspace operates independently. Switching workspaces changes:
- Which brands are visible
- Which projects you can access
- Which credit pool is being used
- Which team members are listed

## A Note on Sub-Workspaces

frndOS does **not currently support sub-workspaces** — meaning you can't have a workspace nested inside another. Each workspace is independent and requires its own subscription.

If you need separation within your team (for example, two sub-brands or two business units), use **brands** and **projects** within a single workspace instead.

👉 See: [Managing Multiple Brands](../brand-setup/multiple-brands)

## FAQ

**Q: Can I be a member of multiple workspaces with one account?**
Yes. One account can belong to multiple workspaces. Use the workspace switcher to move between them.

**Q: I created a workspace by accident. Can I delete it?**
Reach out to your frndOS account representative for help with workspace deletion.

**Q: What happens to my work if I leave a workspace?**
Your contributions stay in the workspace (they belong to the workspace, not your personal account). You lose access but the data remains.

**Q: Can I rename a workspace later?**
Yes — workspace owners and admins can change the name in workspace settings.

**Q: Can two different workspaces share brands or projects?**
Not directly. Brands and projects belong to their workspace. If you need shared access, the workspace admin can invite the relevant people as members or guests.
