# Creating Your Account & First Login

frndOS is now **fully self-serve**. You no longer need to wait for an admin invite to get started — anyone with a work email can sign up directly at [frndos.com](http://frndos.com/).

This guide walks you through creating your account and getting to the dashboard for the first time.

## Three Ways to Sign Up

There are three ways to create a frndOS account:

| Method                  | Email Verification Needed?              | Best For                     |
| ----------------------- | --------------------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| **Email + Password**    | ✅ Yes (OTP code, valid for \~2 minutes) | Users without Google or Lark |
| **Sign in with Google** | ❌ No (auto-verified)                    | Quickest path                |
| **Sign in with Lark**   | ❌ No (auto-verified)                    | Lark-based organizations     |

> **Tip:** If you have a Google or Lark account on your work email, use that — your email is auto-verified and you're in immediately.

## Sign Up with Email + Password

### Step 1 — Open the Sign-Up Page

Go to [frndos.com](http://frndos.com/) and click **Sign Up**.

> **Heads-up:** Use your **work email**, not a personal one. Some organizations restrict access to specific email domains.

### Step 2 — Verify Your Email

After submitting your details, frndOS sends a **verification code (OTP)** to your inbox.

* The code is valid for about **2 minutes** — enter it quickly
* If you don't see it, check your **spam folder** (some org email filters are aggressive)
* The verification link/code is tied to your account for **24 hours**; if you don't verify within that window, your sign-up expires and you'll need to start over

### Step 3 — Complete Your Profile

Once verified, you'll be asked to set up your account basics. Then you'll land in a starting state where you can either:

* **Join an existing workspace** (your agency or organization's space)
* **Create a new workspace** of your own

👉 See: [Joining or Creating a Workspace](./joining-or-creating-workspace)

## Sign Up with Google or Lark

### Step 1 — Choose the Provider

On the sign-up screen, click **Sign in with Google** or **Sign in with Lark**.

### Step 2 — Authorize

You'll be redirected to your Google or Lark account. Confirm the access request.

### Step 3 — You're In

That's it. No OTP, no email check. You're logged in and ready to choose your workspace.

## What You'll See on First Login

When you log in for the first time, frndOS shows your **personal workspace placeholder**. This is just a holding space — frndOS doesn't surface it as your active workspace.

You'll see two main options:

* **Join an existing workspace** — search for your team's workspace and request to join
* **Create a new workspace** — start your own (you'll become the owner)

You won't be able to do creative work yet — frndOS asks you to pick a real workspace first.

## FAQ

**Q: My verification email never arrived. What do I do?**

1. Check your spam/junk folder first
2. If it's not there, your organization's email filters may have blocked it. Reach out to your IT team to whitelist `frndOS` email senders
3. As a workaround, sign up using Google or Lark instead

**Q: I missed the 2-minute OTP window. Can I get a new one?**
Yes — go back to the sign-up flow and request a new code. You don't need to start over from scratch.

**Q: What's the 24-hour window about?**
Your sign-up request is held for 24 hours. If you don't complete email verification within that window, the request expires for security reasons. Just sign up again — same email is fine.

**Q: Can I change my email later?**
Email is a fundamental identifier in frndOS. Reach out to support if you need to change it.

**Q: Why does signing up with Google/Lark skip verification?**
Because those providers have already verified your email identity. frndOS trusts that verification.

**Q: I created an account, but I don't see my agency's workspace yet.**
Your agency's workspace may not be set to "discoverable" by default. Two options:

1. Ask your workspace admin to send you a direct invitation
2. Ask the admin to enable workspace visibility (see [Setting Workspace Visibility](../workspace/workspace-visibility))
