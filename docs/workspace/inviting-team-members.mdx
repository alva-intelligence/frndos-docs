# Inviting Team Members

As a workspace owner or admin, you can invite people to join your workspace directly — without waiting for them to sign up and request access themselves.

This is the right flow for **stakeholders** (clients, brand reps, leadership) who you want to bring in proactively, instead of asking them to discover the workspace on their own.

## When to Invite vs. Let Users Request to Join

| Use **Invite** when… | Use **Request to Join** when… |
|---|---|
| You're proactively adding stakeholders or clients | A new team member should self-serve |
| The user shouldn't have to find the workspace | The user knows the workspace name |
| You want to pre-assign their role | The default role is fine |
| The user doesn't have time to sign up themselves | Discovery is enabled and onboarding is open |

## Before You Start

You need:
- **Owner** or **Admin** role in the workspace
- The **email address** of the person you want to invite
- The **role** you want to assign them

## How to Invite a User

### Step 1 — Open Workspace Settings

1. Open the workspace switcher and confirm you're in the right workspace
2. Go to **Workspace Settings → Members**
3. Click **Invite Member** (or similar)

### Step 2 — Enter Their Details

Provide:
- **Email address** of the person you're inviting
- **Role** you're assigning them (see roles below)
- Optional: a message that goes in the invitation email

### Step 3 — Send

frndOS sends an invitation email to the address you provided.

## What the Invited User Sees

The invitation experience depends on whether the invited user **already has a frndOS account** or not.

### Case 1 — Existing User

If the invited user already has a frndOS account:

1. They get an email saying they've been invited to your workspace
2. They open frndOS and see the invitation as a pending notification
3. They click **Accept** and immediately join the workspace

### Case 2 — New User

If the invited user does not have a frndOS account yet:

1. They get an email inviting them to join
2. Clicking the link takes them to a **sign-up form**
3. The email field is **locked** — they can't change it. This ensures the invitation stays tied to the intended person.
4. They complete sign-up (set password, etc.)
5. After signing up, they're automatically taken to accept the invitation
6. Once accepted, they're inside the workspace

> **Why is the email locked?** Security. The invitation is bound to that specific email address. If users could change it during sign-up, invitations could be transferred to unintended people.

## Roles You Can Assign

When inviting, you assign a role. Roles control what the user can do inside the workspace.

| Role | What They Can Do |
|---|---|
| **Owner** | Full control — workspace settings, billing, member management, all content |
| **Admin** | Manage members, approve requests, manage brands and projects |
| **Editor** | Create and edit content (brands, projects, KVs, etc.) |
| **Viewer / Guest** | View-only access — useful for clients and stakeholders who only need visibility |

> **Note:** Specific role names and capabilities may evolve. Check workspace settings for the current options.

## Inviting Clients & External Stakeholders

For clients or external stakeholders who shouldn't create or edit content:

- Use the **Viewer** or **Guest** role
- This gives them read-only access to the dashboards and assets you've made
- They can't change anything, so there's no risk of accidental edits

## Bulk Inviting

If you have many team members to invite at once, look for the **bulk invite** option in workspace settings (where supported). Otherwise, send invitations one by one — the process is fast.

## FAQ

**Q: What happens if I invite someone who already has a different account?**
The invitation still goes to the email address. They can accept it through their existing account — they don't need a new one.

**Q: Can I revoke an invitation?**
Yes. Pending invitations can be revoked from the workspace members settings before the user accepts.

**Q: Can I change a user's role after they join?**
Yes — workspace admins can change member roles at any time from workspace settings.

**Q: How long does an invitation stay valid?**
Invitations don't typically expire on a short timer like sign-up OTPs, but check workspace settings for current behavior.

**Q: The user I invited never received the email.**
- Have them check spam/junk
- Confirm you sent it to the right email
- Resend the invitation if needed
- If their org email blocks frndOS senders, ask them to whitelist or use a personal email instead

**Q: Can I invite someone to a specific brand, not the whole workspace?**
Brand-level permissions are not currently a granular self-serve feature. Members are added at the workspace level. For client access, use Viewer/Guest role to limit edit rights.
