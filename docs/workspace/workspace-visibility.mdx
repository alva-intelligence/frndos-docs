# Setting Workspace Visibility

By default, **workspaces in frndOS are not discoverable**. This means new users can't find your workspace by searching for it — they have to be directly invited.

If you want to allow team members to self-serve their way in (search and request to join), you need to enable **workspace visibility**.

## When to Enable Visibility

| Enable visibility when… | Keep it disabled when… |
|---|---|
| You're onboarding many new team members | Your team is set and you don't expect new joiners |
| You want self-serve discovery and join requests | You have sensitive client data and want tight control |
| You're a public-facing agency or community | You only want invite-based access |

> **Default behavior:** New workspaces start with visibility **off**. You opt in.

## How to Enable Workspace Visibility

You need **Owner** or **Admin** role.

### Step 1 — Open Workspace Settings

1. Click your workspace name or open the workspace switcher
2. Go to **Workspace Settings**
3. Navigate to the **General** section

### Step 2 — Toggle Visibility On

Find the **Workspace Visibility** toggle in the General settings and switch it on.

### Step 3 — Save

Confirm your changes.

## What Changes When Visibility is On

When visibility is enabled:

- Your workspace **shows up in search results** when other users look for it
- Anyone with a frndOS account can **request to join** your workspace
- Admins receive notifications for every join request
- You're still in control — **users only join after you approve**

Enabling visibility doesn't give immediate access to anyone — it just makes the workspace findable. Approval is still gated.

👉 See: [Approving Join Requests](./approving-join-requests)

## What Changes When Visibility is Off

When visibility is disabled (the default):

- Your workspace **doesn't appear in search results**
- The only way for new users to join is via **direct invitation**
- This is the more secure default for client-sensitive work

If your workspace is invitation-only, make sure all admins know how to invite — see [Inviting Team Members](./inviting-team-members).

## Hybrid Approach: Visibility On + Strict Approval

A useful pattern for agencies: enable visibility so team members can find the workspace, but be **strict about approvals**. This balances:

- **Convenience** for legitimate team members (they can search and request)
- **Security** (you still control who actually joins)

When in doubt, reject the request and re-invite directly.

## A Quick Decision Guide

**If you're a small team where everyone knows everyone:**
- Visibility **off** is fine. Use invitations for all new joiners.

**If you're an agency with rotating team members or freelancers:**
- Visibility **on** with strict approvals usually works best.

**If you're handling sensitive client data:**
- Visibility **off**. Keep all access via direct invitation.

**If you're testing or onboarding many users quickly:**
- Visibility **on** to reduce admin overhead during the initial rollout.

## FAQ

**Q: Will turning visibility on expose any of my data?**
No. Visibility only makes your workspace **searchable by name**. No content, members, or settings become visible to outsiders. They have to be approved and join first.

**Q: Can I toggle visibility off later?**
Yes. You can switch it on and off at any time. Existing members and pending requests are not affected when you toggle it off — but no new requests can come in.

**Q: I turned visibility on but my team still can't find the workspace.**
- Confirm the change was saved
- Have them search by the exact workspace name
- Some search indexing delays are possible — wait a few minutes
- If issues persist, contact frndOS support

**Q: Are there workspace types that can never be made discoverable?**
For enterprise or specialty plans with stricter security requirements, your account representative can advise. By default, the toggle is available to all workspaces.

**Q: Does visibility affect billing or plans?**
No. Visibility is purely a discovery setting and doesn't change your subscription, credits, or features.
