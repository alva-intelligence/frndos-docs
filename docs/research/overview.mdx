# Research Overview

**Research** is where you get to know your audience — before you launch a campaign, or afterwards to measure how it landed. Instead of guessing, you gather real signal and feed it back into your brand work.

Today Research centers on two areas: **Surveys** (run an audience survey end to end) and **Customer Intelligence** (turn your customer list into an enriched, AI-segmented view of your audience) — all without leaving frndOS.

## What's in Research

Research is a growing set of audience tools, each scoped to the brand you're working on:

| Tool | What It Does | Status |
| -------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ | -------------------- |
| **General Survey** | Run audience surveys and collect real responses | Available |
| **Customer Intelligence** | Enrich, segment, and get insights on your customers | Available (gradual rollout) |
| **Brand Health Tracker** | Track brand perception over time | Coming soon |
| **Persona Generator** | Build audience personas from your data | Coming soon |
| **A/B Testing** | Test creative variants against your audience | Coming soon |

> More Research tools are rolling out. Stay tuned via the [What's New](/blog) section.

## Where to Find Research

1. Log in to **frndOS**
2. From Home, click the brand you're working on under **Brands** in the sidebar
3. Once inside the brand, open **Research** in the sidebar
4. Pick the tool you need — for example, **General Survey**

> **Why select the brand first?** Research tools are brand-aware — every survey you run is filed under whichever brand is active in the sidebar. Picking the right brand before you start keeps your results with the correct brand.

## Per-Brand & Team-Only

Research is **scoped per brand** and only available in a **team workspace**:

- Every survey belongs to a brand — the **Client** column on the Surveys list shows which one.
- Research isn't available in a **Personal** workspace. If you're in one, frndOS sends you back to Home.
- If you handle multiple brands, switch between them via **Back to Home**, then pick another brand under **Brands**.

👉 See: [Working with Multiple Brands](../brand-setup/multiple-brands)

## Getting Started with Surveys

To run your first survey for a brand:

1. ✅ **Open the right brand** and go to **Research** → [Creating a General Survey](./creating-a-general-survey)
2. ✅ **Track it** as responses come in → [Managing & Tracking Your Surveys](./managing-surveys)
3. ✅ **File it under a brand and share it** with your team → [Assigning a Brand & Sharing Surveys](./assigning-brand-and-sharing)

> **Heads-up:** Surveys are paid from your credit balance, and you'll need survey access on your role. See [Managing Workspace Credits](../workspace/managing-credits) and [Inviting Team Members](../workspace/inviting-team-members).

## Getting Started with Customer Intelligence

To understand your customer base for a brand:

1. ✅ **Open the right brand** and open Customer Intelligence → [Customer Intelligence Overview](./customer-intelligence-overview)
2. ✅ **Import & enrich** your customers from a CSV → [Importing & Enriching Customers](./importing-customers)
3. ✅ **Explore** customers and segments → [Exploring Customers & Segments](./exploring-customers-and-segments)
4. ✅ **Get insights** on a filtered audience → [Getting Customer Insights](./customer-insights)

> **Heads-up:** Customer Intelligence rolls out gradually behind a feature flag, and enrichment is paid from your credit balance.

## FAQ

**Q: I don't see Research, or the tools are greyed out.**
Research is team-only and permission-gated. Make sure you're in a team workspace (not Personal), and that your role has survey access — ask a workspace admin if unsure.

**Q: Which brand do my surveys belong to?**
The brand that was active when you created them. You can reassign a survey to another brand later. See [Assigning a Brand & Sharing Surveys](./assigning-brand-and-sharing).

**Q: When are the other Research tools coming?**
Brand Health Tracker, Persona Generator, and A/B Testing are on the roadmap. Watch the [What's New](/blog) section for release announcements.
