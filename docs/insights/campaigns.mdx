# Creating & Managing Campaigns in Insights

The **Campaign** tab in Insights gives you **filtered, campaign-specific performance reports** — separate from the always-on data you see in the Paid Media and Owned Media tabs.

While Paid Media and Owned Media tabs show **everything** (every ad, every post), the Campaign tab shows **only what's tagged or filtered as part of a specific campaign**.

## What the Campaign Tab is For

Use the Campaign tab when you need to:

- Report on a **specific campaign's** performance
- Compare **campaign vs. always-on** performance
- Generate **stakeholder-ready summaries** for individual campaigns
- See performance over a **campaign timeline** (with start and end dates)

## How Campaigns Get Their Data

Campaign reports pull data from your existing connected sources (Paid Media, Owned Media, etc.), filtered by the tags you've applied to posts and ads.

This is why **tagging matters**. Without tags, you can't isolate campaign performance.

👉 See: [Understanding Owned Media & Content Labels](./owned-media)

## Creating a Campaign Report

> **Note:** The exact creation UI is evolving. The general workflow below reflects the current direction.

1. Navigate to **Insights → Campaign**
2. Click **Create Campaign** (or similar)
3. Define:
   - **Campaign name**
   - **Date range** (start and end)
   - **Tags / filters** that identify this campaign's content
4. Save — frndOS aggregates matching posts and ads under this campaign view

## What You'll See in a Campaign Report

A campaign report typically includes:

- **Headline KPIs** — impressions, reach, engagement, spend (depending on data sources)
- **Funnel breakdown** — awareness / consideration / conversion contribution
- **Top-performing content** — best posts and ads in the campaign
- **Timeline view** — performance over the campaign duration

## Campaign Timeline

One of the most useful features is the **campaign timeline** — a visual view showing how performance moved over the campaign's run.

Future direction: timelines will integrate with **social listening data**, so you can see things like:
- *"ATL launch on Day 3 → conversation spike on Day 4"*
- *"Influencer post on Day 10 → engagement bump across owned channels"*

This helps connect cause and effect across your media mix.

## Standard Labels for Campaign Reports

For best results, make sure your content is tagged with these standard labels (at minimum):

- **Campaign** — the campaign name
- **Content Pillar** — which brand pillar
- **Objective** — funnel stage (awareness / consideration / conversion)

These labels feed directly into the report structure.

👉 See: [Understanding Owned Media & Content Labels](./owned-media)

## Auto-Generated Reports (Roadmap)

Future direction: **automatic monthly and quarterly campaign reports**.

The plan:
1. You set up a campaign with template + tags once
2. Every month (or quarter), frndOS auto-generates the report
3. You get a notification: *"Your monthly report is ready."*
4. Review, approve, share

This moves campaign reporting from manual to proactive — eliminating the recurring monthly report grind.

> **Status:** This is a planned capability. Current campaign reports are manual.

## FAQ

**Q: What's the difference between the Campaign tab and the Paid/Owned tabs?**
- **Paid Media / Owned Media tabs** = always-on data (everything)
- **Campaign tab** = filtered subset for a specific campaign

**Q: Can I include multiple media types in one campaign report?**
Yes. A campaign report can aggregate tagged content from Paid Media, Owned Media, Earned Media, and ATL — as long as those items are tagged with the campaign.

**Q: How does frndOS know which content belongs to a campaign?**
Via tags you apply (e.g., `Campaign = Summer Launch`). Without tags, content can't be assigned to a campaign report.

**Q: Can I export a campaign report?**
Excel export is supported. Google Slides export is on the roadmap.

**Q: Can I customize the campaign report template?**
Custom report templates with defined narrative structure are part of the upcoming **report builder** flow.
