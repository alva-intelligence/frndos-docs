# Managing Multiple Brands

If you're working at an agency, chances are you handle more than one brand. frndOS is designed for this — each brand has **independent Brand Knowledge, projects, and assets**, all accessible from a single account.

## How Multiple Brands Work

When you log in to frndOS, all brands assigned to your account appear under **Brands** in the Home sidebar.

Each brand has its own:
- **Brand Knowledge** (DNA, Identity, Tonality)
- **Projects** and campaigns
- **Generated assets** and history
- **Brand-specific templates**

## Entering and Switching Brands

1. From the **Home** sidebar, click a brand under **Brands**
2. The sidebar transforms to that brand's context — Pillars (Studio, Insights, Research, Growth, Loyalty), brand settings, and brand-scoped Chat
3. Everything (projects, assets, Brand Knowledge) updates to that brand's context

From inside a brand you can open its **brand settings**, jump into a pillar (Studio, Insights), or review its assets — all scoped to that brand.

**To switch to a different brand**, click **Back to Home** at the top of the sidebar, then pick another brand from the **Brands** list.

👉 See: [Navigating frndOS — The Sidebar](../getting-started/navigating-frndos) for the full breakdown of the Home vs. Brand sidebar.

## Creating a New Brand

Creating a brand is a more deliberate flow than switching to one. Rather than a single click, brand creation is being given a proper guided setup so your Brand Knowledge is configured correctly from the start.

> **Who can create a brand?** Only **workspace admins** (and owners) can create new brands. If you're a regular member and don't see the option to add a brand, ask your workspace admin to create it — once it exists, you can be given access to work on it.

Once a brand exists, **any member with access to it** can set up its Brand Knowledge — DNA, Identity, and Tone of Voice don't require admin privileges. Plan to configure [Brand DNA](./brand-profile), [Brand Identity](./brand-identity), and [Tone of Voice](./tone-of-voice) as part of onboarding the brand — that's what makes everything downstream work.

> **Heads-up:** The exact brand-creation entry point and steps are evolving. Look for the **add brand** option in the **Brands** list on Home, and expect a short setup flow rather than an instant create.

## Best Practices

### Set Up Brand Knowledge for Each Brand Separately

Don't reuse Brand Knowledge across brands. Each brand should have its own DNA, Identity, and Tonality configured independently — even if some elements (like target audience) overlap.

👉 See: [Setting Up Your Brand Profile](./brand-profile)

### Use Projects to Keep Campaigns Organized

Within each brand, use Projects to separate different campaigns. This keeps briefs, KVs, and references organized by campaign — not just by brand.

👉 See: [Creating & Managing Projects](../projects/managing-projects)

### Be Mindful of Active Brand Context

Always check which brand is selected before generating. Brand context determines:
- Which Brand Knowledge applies
- Where generated assets are saved
- Which projects you can see

## Combining Data Across Brands (Roll-Up Brands)

If you manage several related brands (for example, multiple sub-brands under one corporate parent), you can build a **roll-up brand** that aggregates performance data from the others — without re-connecting or duplicating data.

This is done in Insights using **Extended connections**, which reuse data already connected to other brands in your workspace.

**Example:** Create a "Home Care" brand and pull in the existing connections from Sunlight, Rinso, and Pepsodent to see combined performance — no duplicate syncing, no extra cost.

👉 See: [Core vs Extended Connections](../insights/core-extended-connections)

> **Note:** Data brought in via Extended connection is read-only from the roll-up brand. Managing or removing it must be done from the original brand that owns the connection.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I add a new brand to my workspace?**
Brand creation is **admin-only** — look for the **add brand** option in the **Brands** list on Home if you're a workspace admin or owner. If you're a regular member, ask your admin to create the brand. Brand creation uses a short guided setup so Brand Knowledge can be configured from the start. See [Creating a New Brand](#creating-a-new-brand) above.

**Q: Do I need to be an admin to set up Brand Knowledge?**
No. Once a brand exists and you have access to it, any member can configure its Brand DNA, Brand Identity, and Tone of Voice. The admin-only restriction applies to *creating* the brand, not to setting up its knowledge.

**Q: Can I copy Brand Knowledge from one brand to another?**
This is not currently a standard self-serve feature. Reach out to your frndOS team if you have a specific use case.

**Q: Can multiple team members access the same brand?**
Yes. Brand access is managed through workspace settings and permissions.

**Q: What happens if I generate a KV under the wrong brand?**
You can recreate the KV under the correct brand. There's no automatic move feature between brands — Brand Knowledge differences would make a direct transfer unreliable anyway.
